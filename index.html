The Problem

Student mental health is at a breaking point.

  • Reilly (2018) reports record numbers of students seeking treatment for depression and anxiety.
  • Counseling centers across the country cannot meet current demand.
  • Existing guidance tends to be either too abstract ("be mindful") or too demanding ("overhaul your life").
  • Students need interventions they can adopt now, without waiting for institutional capacity to scale.

Our Approach

Three Small Changes

Drawn from course readings, designed for daily use.

01
Phone Boundaries
Two phone-free windows each day: one for focused work, one in the hour before sleep.
02
Three Good Things
Each evening, record three positive moments from the day. Over time, this shifts what we notice.
03
Sleep & Movement
A consistent sleep window paired with thirty minutes of daily walking.
The Research

The Evidence

Each change is supported by research assigned in this course.

01
Phone Boundaries
The Practice
  • 1 hr phone-off during deep work
  • 1 hr phone-free before bed
Twenge et al. (2018) found that adolescent well-being declined sharply after 2012, the year smartphone adoption crossed fifty percent. Brown and Ryan (2003) link present-moment attention to higher reported well-being.
02
Three Good Things
The Practice
  • Each night: 3 good things + why each mattered
  • Under 5 minutes
Seligman, Steen, Park, and Peterson (2005) found that one week of this exercise produced gains in happiness that persisted for up to six months.
03
Sleep & Movement
The Practice
  • Consistent sleep window (30 min)
  • 30 minutes of daily movement
Ratey and Loehr (2011) report that regular physical activity improves cognition, mood regulation, and stress response.
The challenge is consistency, not awareness.
